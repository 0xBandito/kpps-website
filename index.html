About Dr. Kraska

Dr. Jenny
Kraska

Licensed Clinical & Sport Psychologist

Dr. Kraska spent 8 years embedded inside elite collegiate athletics departments — Georgia Tech, University of Arizona, Washington State, and Arizona State — providing mental health and performance services to student-athletes competing at the highest collegiate level.

She was also a college athlete herself. Soccer team captain at two schools. She understands what it takes to compete because she has done it.

Now in private practice in her hometown of Atlanta, Dr. Kraska brings that same level of specialized expertise to individual athletes, teams, and high performers ready to close the gap between where they are and where they know they can be.

  • Psy.D., Clinical Psychology — California Lutheran University
  • Licensed Psychologist — Georgia & Arizona
  • PSYPACT Member — Authorized in 38 States
  • Pre-doctoral Internship — Arizona State University Athletics
  • Postdoctoral Fellowship — Washington State University Athletics

Approach

How Dr. Kraska Works

Every session is built around you — your sport, your challenges, your goals. Five principles guide every engagement.

01

Person-Centered

The therapeutic relationship comes first. Sessions are a genuine partnership — built on trust, not a template.

02

Evidence-Based

All work is grounded in proven frameworks — Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT) and Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) — applied practically to performance and wellbeing.

03

Trauma-Informed

Sessions are built on safety, choice, and empowerment — with full understanding of how past experiences shape how you perform and feel today.

04

Strength-Based

The focus is on what you already have and building from there — not cataloguing deficits. You have more than you think.

05

Performance-Focused

Every skill developed in session has direct application in competition, in the boardroom, or wherever your performance happens.
