Background

8 Years Inside
Elite Athletics

Licensed Clinical & Sport Psychologist

Dr. Kraska spent 8 years embedded inside elite collegiate athletics departments — Georgia Tech, University of Arizona, Washington State University, and Arizona State University — providing mental health and performance services to student-athletes competing at the highest collegiate level. She knows the culture, the pressure, and the psychology of high performance from the inside.

Before any of that, she was a college athlete herself. Soccer team captain at two schools — Saddleback College and California Lutheran University. She understands what it takes to compete because she has done it.

Dr. Kraska earned her Psy.D. in Clinical Psychology from California Lutheran University, where she specialized in working with athletes throughout her training. She completed her pre-doctoral internship at Arizona State University's Counseling Services and her postdoctoral fellowship at Washington State University Athletics, serving as the lead provider for the entire athletics department.

Now in private practice in her hometown of Atlanta, Dr. Kraska brings that same level of specialized expertise to individual athletes, teams, and high performers ready to close the gap between where they are and where they know they can be.

Clinical Approach

How Dr. Kraska
Works

Every session is built around you — your sport, your challenges, your goals. Five principles guide every engagement.

01

Person-Centered

The therapeutic relationship comes first. Sessions are a genuine partnership built on trust and collaboration — not a prescription handed down from authority.

02

Evidence-Based

All work is grounded in proven frameworks — Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT) and Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) — applied practically to performance and wellbeing rather than applied theoretically.

03

Trauma-Informed

Sessions are built on safety, choice, and empowerment — with full understanding of how past experiences shape how you perform and how you feel in the present moment.

04

Strength-Based

The focus is on what you already have and building from there — not cataloguing deficits or limitations. You bring more to this work than you think.

05

Performance-Focused

Every skill developed in session has direct application — in competition, in the boardroom, or wherever your performance happens. Mental health and peak performance are not in conflict.
